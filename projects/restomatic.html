Overview

Restomatic is a full-stack restaurant management platform: menus, orders, and operations on one side, paired with a standout feature — an AI Food Finder that identifies a dish from a photo. Built with a teammate, split cleanly across backend/AI and frontend.

My role: AI Food Finder

The feature takes a photo of food and returns the closest matching menu item, combining image recognition with fuzzy text matching to handle cases where visual recognition alone isn't confident enough:

1# food_finder.py
2def find_dish(image):
3 candidates = image_recognition(image)
4 if confidence(candidates) < threshold:
5 candidates = fuzzy_match(candidates, menu_db)
6 return best_match(candidates)

On the backend side, I built the Flask API layer connecting the Flutter frontend to MySQL for menu/order data, and the AI service handling the image-to-dish matching pipeline.

Challenges

Real-world photos are messy
Plating, lighting, and camera angle vary a lot between users. Image recognition alone wasn't reliable enough, which is why a fuzzy-matching fallback against the menu database was necessary to catch ambiguous cases.
Coordinating across a two-person split
With one person on backend/AI and the other on frontend, keeping the API contract stable while both sides were still actively changing required clear, early agreement on request/response shapes.
Getting a multi-service app actually deployed
Shipping a live deployment — not just a local build — meant making sure the Flutter app, Flask API, and MySQL database were all reachable and correctly configured together in production.
